AGE OF ENLIGHTENMENT

After the Middle Ages, which was dominated by the church, a new era of human civilization appeared in all spheres of life. This brought big changes in production and social system, as a result of which new layers in society emerge and civil revolutions break out.

In the seventeenth century there was a growth of industrialization; cities expanded and new classes appeared in society. The first attempts were made to improve the position of a woman in society. At the time, they were very rare and mostly related to the position of women in the family.

Significant changes in society occurred in the 18th century. In the middle of this century, a group of enlightened thinkers began to oppose the tyranny of feudal societies based on the hereditary privileges of kings, the church, and the nobility. Until then, the "divine right" of kings was opposed by these thinkers to a new value - the "right of man". They express the dissatisfaction of a new and growing middle class that craves progress and which is overwhelmed by the old, rigid and corrupt feudal hierarchy. It was the beginning of the Enlightenment. In this turmoil,women begin to question their inequality and to oppose the tyranny of the men in the house.
